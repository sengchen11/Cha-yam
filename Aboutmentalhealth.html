About Mental Health

What is Mental Health

Mental health is our emotional, psychological and social well-being. It affects how we think,feel and act.Good mental health is when you feel positive about yourself and cope well with it in your everyday pressures.Mental health is important at every stage of life from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

According to World Health Organization (WHO), Mental health includes subjective well-being, perceived self-efficacy, autonomy, competence, intergenerational dependence, and self-actualization of one's intellectual and emotional potential, among others.From the perspectives of positive psychology or holism, mental health may include an individual's ability to enjoy life and to create a balance between life activities and efforts to achieve psychological resilience.Cultural differences, subjective assessments, and competing professional theories all affect how one defines "mental health".Some early signs related health problems are sleep irritation, lack of energy and suicidal thinking.

Medication, counselling or both can help you manage mental illness.

Early warning signs of Mental Health

  • Changes in appetite
  • Difficulty sleeping or sleeping too much
  • Helplessness and hopelessness
  • Social withdrawal
  • Severe mood swings
  • Trouble in your relationships, like increased fighting or yelling
  • Inability to perform everyday tasks
  • Lack of interest in people or activities you love
  • Thinking of harming yourself or others

Types of mental health issues and illness

  • Anxiety disorders
  • Behavioural and emotional disorders in children
  • Bipolar affective disorder
  • Depression
  • Dissociation and dissociative disorders
  • Eating disorders
  • Obsessive compulsive disorder(OCD)
  • Paranoia
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder
  • Psychosis
  • Schizophrenia

Ways to recover your Mental Health

  • Remind yourself that you are not alone
  • Pay attention to your body as well as your mind
  • Take care of your body — even when (especially when) you don’t feel like it
  • If your doctor prescribes medication, take it as prescribed
  • Go to therapy
  • Connect with the people around you
  • Be active
  • Give to others as acts of kindness can improve your mood and have positive impact on you
  • Keep learning
